Worlebury Woods

Worlebury Woods, also known as Weston Woods can be found on the northern end of Weston-super-Mare high on the hillside which overlooks Weston and beyond.

It is a splendid place to visit if you seek tranquil walks amongst nature. The dense canopy of trees is perfect for those wishing to find a quieter spot away from the hustle and bustle of the town. It is favoured locally by dog owners whose canines can enjoy their own stick finding adventures.

Meandering pathways which are well sign posted and accessible for wheelchairs, take you on a glorious journey. Depending what time of the year you visit, you'll be rewarded by plenty of birdsong, flora and fauna to admire.

Children are catered for with a wooden play area, including a zip wire, which adults can enjoy too! Bring along a pinic to enjoy in the designated area.

Worlebury Woods has an exciting history, with more information to be found here or by popping into Weston Museum to learn more about Iron Age settlers that were here over 2000 years ago.

Seafront & Beach

Known for having one of the highest tidal ranges in the world, Weston-super-Mare beach and seafront are popular destinations for many. The long, sandy beaches stretch along the Bristol Channel from the promenade, where visitors can enjoy views across the water to Steep Holm.

The beach is host to various events throughout the year, including the famous beach race. The Tropicana, found in prime position along the seafront was also chosen as Banksy's 'Dismaland'.

The beach donkeys are synonymous with Weston-super-Mare and offer families an exciting trip out.

Dogs are also able to enjoy the beach, but restrictions do apply at certain times during the year.

There is easy access to the high street, which offers places to eat and drink, as well as shopping.

The Grand Pier

With an impressive history, the Grand Pier was first conceived in the 1800's, before construction started in 1903.

It offered itself as a venue for music, comedy, plays and performances, and still hosts many exciting events to this day.

The Grand Pier has gone through various changes over the years, surviving catastrophic fires.

Its a historical landmark which people from all over the world come to visit.

Many enjoy the amusements, with a huge variety on offer for all ages.
